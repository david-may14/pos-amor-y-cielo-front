# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Young adults (Gen Z / young millennials) arriving from Instagram or TikTok, already pre-sold on the aesthetic. They discovered the café through a reel or a post and came to the site to confirm the vibe, check hours and location, browse the menu, and decide whether to visit tonight. Discovery is already done — the site's job is conversion: turn curiosity into a visit.

## Product Purpose

Marketing website for Amor & Cielo Cafetería, a specialty café in Mérida, Yucatán. Open Monday through Saturday, 5:30–10:00 pm. Offers specialty coffee drinks, artisanal bakery (sourdough, cinnamon rolls, cookies), and light food. The site exists to make someone who lands from a social media referral feel the warmth of the place — and immediately know when and where to go.

Success: a visitor leaves with hours, address, and a genuine desire to walk through the door.

## Brand Personality

Cálido, artesanal, íntimo. Warm, handcrafted, intimate. The neighborhood café that feels like a secret worth keeping. Evening energy — quiet, unhurried, a little candlelit. The antidote to the noise of the day.

Voice: warm and personal without being folksy. Speaks in the second person, close but not intrusive. Copy is spare — every sentence earns its place.

## Anti-references

- **Over-designed artisan cliché**: kraft paper textures, mason jar illustrations, chalkboard fonts, "locally sourced" stamped on everything. Looks handmade in a manufactured way.
- **Generic restaurant template**: stock fork-and-knife icons, yellow-and-red palettes, repetitive layout patterns. No soul, no specificity.
- **Starbucks-style corporate café**: polished but soulless. Green-and-white brand system, stock photo smiles, nothing that could only be this place.
- **Loud social media aesthetic**: bright pinks, neon gradients, trendy-for-six-months visual decisions. Noisy when the brand is quiet.

## Design Principles

1. **Quiet confidence.** Let the craft speak without announcing itself. No loud claims, no persuasive push. The work earns trust through restraint, not volume.

2. **Evening atmosphere.** The site should feel like arriving at 7 pm, not 9 am. Warm light, unhurried pace. Design decisions should pass the test: does this feel like stepping inside the café at dusk?

3. **Photography carries the brand.** The audience came for the vibe. Images tell the story faster than copy. Show the place — don't describe it.

4. **Intimate scale.** Resist the urge to fill space. Whitespace is welcome. Things should feel considered, not crowded. Blue Bottle's editorial restraint is the reference: craft signals without clutter.

5. **Specificity over generality.** Mérida, Barrio de Santiago, 5:30 pm, masa madre — these details make the brand real. Anything that could belong to any café belongs to none.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Aesthetic-first. No strict WCAG level mandated. Maintain reasonable baseline: readable contrast for body text, keyboard-navigable links, descriptive alt text on images. Reduced motion is not a priority for this audience.
